##### Tsiolkovsky's rocket equation

<br>

<div style="text-align:center"><em>"Earth is the cradle of humanity, but one cannot remain in the cradle forever."</em></div>

<br>

From conservation of momentum, change in momentum of rocket = minus momentum of exhaust. Let wet mass of rocket at time $t$ be $M(t)$, initial wet mass = $M_w$, dry mass = $M_d$, exhaust momentum = $dp$, propellant flow rate = $\dot{m}$, exhaust velocity = $v_e$ and change in time = $dt$. We have,

$M(t)\;dv=-dp\implies M(t)\;dv=-v_e\;\dot{m}\;dt$, and since $M(t)=M_w-\dot{m}\;t$, we have $dv=\frac{-v_e\;\dot{m}\;dt}{M_w\;-\;\dot{m}\;t}$,

integrating LHS and RHS from $t=0$ to $t=\frac{P}{\dot{m}}$, 

$\int dv=\int_{0}^{\frac{P}{\dot{m}}} \frac{v_e\;\dot{m}\;dt}{M_w\;-\;\dot{m}\;t}=v_e\;[ln(M_w-\dot{m}\;t)]_{\frac{P}{\dot{m}}}^{0}\implies \Delta v=v_e\;ln(\frac{M_w}{M_d})$,

which is Tsiolkovsky's rocket equation.
